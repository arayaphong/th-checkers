# **Thai Checkers Game Rules**

Complete rules for Thai Checkers (หมากฮอส), covering the distinct mechanics
for both Pion (หมาก) and Dame (ฮอส) piece types.

## **1. Game Overview**

Thai Checkers (หมากฮอส) is played on a standard 8x8 checkerboard,
utilizing only the 32 dark squares. Each player commands an army of 8 pieces
(Pions) positioned on the first two rows of their respective sides. The
primary objective is to either capture all of the opponent's pieces or trap
them so they cannot make any legal moves.

## **3. Piece Mechanics - Pion**

### **Movement**

The Pion is strictly limited to moving **diagonally forward**, one square at
a time, into an adjacent empty square.

* **Black Pions** move diagonally downward (increasing row numbers).  
* **White Pions** move diagonally upward (decreasing row numbers).  
* Pions cannot move backward.

### **Capturing**

* **The Mechanism:** If an opponent's piece is diagonally adjacent in a
  forward direction and the square immediately behind it is empty, the Pion
  **must** capture it. The capturing Pion jumps over the opponent and lands on
  that immediate empty square, removing the captured piece from the board.  
* **Directional Limit:** Pions may **only** capture in a forward diagonal direction.  
* **Multiple Captures (Chaining):** If another forward capture is available
  from the landing square, the Pion **must** continue jumping in the same
  turn. This chain continues until no further forward captures are possible.

### **Promotion**

* When a Pion successfully reaches the opponent's back row (row 1 for White,
  row 8 for Black), it is immediately **promoted to a Dame**.  
* **Crucial Rule:** The player's turn **ends immediately upon promotion**. Even
  if the newly promoted Dame is positioned to make a capture, it cannot do so
  until the player's next turn. It gains full Dame abilities starting on the
  subsequent turn.

## **4. Piece Mechanics - Dame**

### **Movement**

A Dame possesses "flying" movement capabilities. It can move diagonally in
**all four directions** (both forward and backward) across **any number of
empty squares** in a single move.

### **Capturing**

* **The Mechanism (Long-Range Capture):** A Dame **must** capture an
  opponent's piece if it lies anywhere on an unobstructed diagonal path
  (meaning there are no blocking pieces between the Dame and the target), AND
  there is an empty square immediately beyond the opponent.  
* **Landing Restriction:** The Dame slides along the diagonal, jumps the
  opponent's piece, and **must land on the very first empty square**
  immediately after the jumped piece. It cannot slide further down the empty
  diagonal post-capture. The captured piece is then removed.  
* **Multiple Captures (Chaining):** If another capture is available from that
  exact landing square, the Dame **must** continue capturing. During each jump
  in the sequence, it must always land on the first available empty square
  beyond the jumped piece.

## **5. Core Gameplay Rules**

### **The Mandatory Capture Rule**

If a capture is legally possible on the board, **it must be taken**. A player
cannot opt to make a standard non-capturing move when a jump is available.

* **Choice of Sequence:** If multiple distinct capture sequences are available
  at the start of a turn, the player is free to choose which sequence to
  execute. There is no rule requiring the player to choose the sequence that
  captures the maximum number of pieces.

### **Turn Sequence**

1. **White (Player 1)** always moves first.  
2. Players alternate turns.  
3. A turn consists of exactly one of the following:  
   * A single regular move.  
   * A capture sequence (which may involve multiple chained jumps).  
4. As noted, a turn ends immediately if a Pion is promoted.

## **6. Winning and Drawing Conditions**

**Winning the Game:**

A player achieves victory under either of these conditions:

1. **Elimination:** Capturing all of the opponent's pieces.  
2. **Domination (Blocking):** The opponent still has pieces on the board, but
  they are entirely blocked from making any legal moves.

**Drawing the Game:**

A game results in a draw (tie) when:

1. **Looping Positions:** Players continuously repeat positions without
  progressing toward an ending condition (Note: This document does not define
  a fixed mathematical threshold for automatic draws via repetition).  
2. **Mutual Agreement:** Both players voluntarily agree to a draw.

## **9. Summary**

Thai Checkers pairs straightforward mechanics with deep strategic routing:

* **Pions** push forward one square at a time and capture forward.  
* **Dames** (promoted Pions) gain unlimited diagonal sliding but must land
  immediately behind a captured piece.  
* **Captures are strictly mandatory**, forcing tactical sacrifices.  
* **Promotions immediately end the turn**, delaying the Dame's power until the next move.  
* Victory is achieved via **elimination or complete blocking**, with endless looping resulting in a draw.
